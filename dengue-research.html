Essential Prevention Tips

Eliminate Standing Water

Remove or cover water storage containers, flowerpots, and any items that can collect rainwater.

Use Protective Measures

Apply mosquito repellents, use bed nets, and wear long-sleeved clothing during peak hours.

Maintain Clean Surroundings

Keep your environment clean, ensure proper drainage, and regularly clean water tanks.

Avoid Peak Hours

Limit outdoor activities during dawn and dusk when Aedes mosquitoes are most active.

Monitor Symptoms

Watch for fever, headache, muscle pain, and rash. Seek immediate medical attention if symptoms appear.

Community Awareness

Educate family and neighbors about prevention measures and work together for community-wide control.

Key Takeaways

Monsoon Vigilance

Stay extra vigilant during monsoon months when 85% of dengue cases occur. Implement prevention measures proactively.

Early Detection

Early detection and treatment can improve outcomes by 80%. Don't ignore fever during dengue season.

Community Effort

Dengue prevention requires community-wide effort. Share knowledge and work together for effective control.
